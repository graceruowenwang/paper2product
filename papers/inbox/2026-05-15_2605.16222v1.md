## 摘要

Aphasias, selective language impairments which can arise from brain damage, reveal the functional organization of human language by providing causal links between affected brain regions and specific symptom profiles. Drawing on this literature, we introduce an aphasia-inspired technique to characterize the emergent functional organization of language models (LMs). We ``lesion'' (zero-out) model parameters and measure the effects of this intervention against clinical aphasia symptoms, as diagnosed by the Text Aphasia Battery (TAB).
